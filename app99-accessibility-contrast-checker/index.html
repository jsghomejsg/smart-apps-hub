The Mathematics of Digital Inclusion

Web accessibility is calculated using the relative luminance of colors. Our 2026 Contrast Checker follows the WCAG 2.2 guidelines to ensure your digital products are usable by people with visual impairments or color blindness. The contrast ratio is calculated using the following formula:

$$Contrast = \frac{L1 + 0.05}{L2 + 0.05}$$

Where $L1$ is the relative luminance of the lighter color and $L2$ is the relative luminance of the darker color. To pass Level AA, a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 is required for normal text. Achieving Level AAA requires a more stringent ratio of 7:1. Using this professional UX tool ensures your design meets international standards for readability.
